Sleep
While napping, a cat/dog should respond quickly to external stimuli, such as the sound of a door opening, the smell of food, or sudden noises. Understanding a animal's normal sleep habits is important, as many serious illnesses begin with abnormal lethargy.
Diet
A decrease in appetite may indicate dental or oral issues, or a serious illness. A sudden increase in appetite could signal diabetes or hyperthyroidism. Increased water intake may also indicate kidney disease or diabetes.
Eyes
Healthy animal should have bright eyes with no tearing, no discharge, and both eyes should be of equal size.
Ears
The ears should be clean and dry to the touch on the inside, appearing a clean pink color. The ears should stand upright (except for Scottish Fold animals), with little to no earwax or foreign matter. A healthy cat will respond sensitively to its owner's calls or other sounds when in a quiet state, moving its ears back and forth upon hearing noises. Sick or deaf cats may show weak or no response to sounds.
Nose
The front of the nose should be moist and cool, with no excessive discharge. A dry nose may indicate a feverish condition (such as an infectious disease). A animal's nose is usually dry while sleeping.
Mouth
The area around the mouth should be clean and dry, with minimal drooling, no saliva or food residue, and no bad breath. The gums, tongue, and palate should be pink, and the teeth should be white or slightly yellow, with no missing teeth.
Limbs
Observe the animal's posture while walking or running. If there is limping or difficulty moving, it may indicate skeletal issues. Visible "lumps" or "bumps" on the limbs could be tumors or "vaccine-associated sarcoma" from vaccinations.
Skin
The skin should be soft and elastic, with a moderate temperature that feels warm to the touch. The coat should be dense, soft, and shiny, with no bald patches, redness, or lumps. There should be no parasites like fleas or ticks.
Body Shape
A healthy animal should have a well-proportioned body and feel solid when picked up. If you can easily feel the ribs or spine along the back, the cat may be underweight or severely thin
Bahavior
A healthy animal will respond quickly during play, with kittens being particularly reactive and agile.
